⏰ 8:00 AM · Breakfast
Veg Masala Oats + 3 Boiled Eggs
₹45
430kcal
28gProtein
45gCarbs
14gFat
Ingredients
50g oats3 eggs½ onion½ tomatogreen chilliturmeric + salt1 tsp oil
How to prepare
  1. Put 3 eggs in a pot, cover with water, boil 8–10 min. Cool, peel.
  2. Heat 1 tsp oil in a pan. Add chopped onion, chilli — sauté 2 min.
  3. Add chopped tomato, pinch of turmeric and salt. Cook 2 min until soft.
  4. Add 50g oats + 1 cup water. Stir and simmer 4–5 min until thick.
  5. Eat the oats hot, with the 3 boiled eggs on the side.
Tip: Eat 2 whole eggs + 1 egg if you want lower fat — egg whites are pure protein. Boil 6 eggs at once and refrigerate to save time.
⏰ 1:00 PM · Lunch
Grilled Chicken + Rice + Dal
₹85
580kcal
52gProtein
62gCarbs
12gFat
Ingredients
150g chicken breast60g rice (raw)40g dal/moongcurd 100gginger-garlicspices1 tsp oil
How to prepare
  1. Cut 150g chicken breast into pieces. Marinate 15 min in curd, ginger-garlic, salt, chilli powder, turmeric.
  2. Use your oven: bake at 200°C for 18–20 min, OR pan-grill with 1 tsp oil until cooked through (no pink inside).
  3. Cook 60g rice in 1.5 cups water until soft (~15 min).
  4. Boil 40g dal with water, turmeric, salt until soft; temper with a little garlic.
  5. Plate the rice, dal, and grilled chicken together. Eat warm.
Tip: Chicken breast is the cheapest protein per gram. Buy 1 kg, portion into 150g packs and freeze — pull one out the night before.
⏰ 5:00 PM · Pre/Post Workout Snack
Curd + Banana + Roasted Chana
₹35
320kcal
18gProtein
48gCarbs
6gFat
Ingredients
200g curd1 banana40g roasted chana
How to prepare
  1. Scoop 200g plain curd into a bowl.
  2. Slice 1 banana into it.
  3. Eat the roasted chana (roasted chickpeas) on the side as a crunchy protein hit.
  4. Have this ~45 min before the gym for energy, or right after for recovery.
Tip: Roasted chana is dirt cheap, shelf-stable, and high protein. Keep a packet in your gym bag.
⏰ 8:30 PM · Dinner
Egg Bhurji + 2 Roti + Salad
₹50
420kcal
26gProtein
40gCarbs
16gFat
Ingredients
3 eggs2 wheat rotionion + tomatocucumbergreen chillispices1 tsp oil
How to prepare
  1. Heat 1 tsp oil. Sauté chopped onion, chilli, then tomato + turmeric + salt.
  2. Crack in 3 eggs, scramble and stir-cook 3–4 min until fully done — that's egg bhurji.
  3. Make or reheat 2 wheat rotis.
  4. Chop cucumber, onion, tomato into a quick salad with a pinch of salt and lemon.
  5. Eat the bhurji with roti and salad on the side.
Tip: Keep dinner carbs light. Swap to 1 roti if you want a steeper deficit on rest days.

RULES & SWAPS
Keep it simple, keep it consistent

✅ Daily Rules

💧
Water: 3–4 litres a day. Often "hunger" is thirst. Drink a glass before each meal.
🍳
Protein every meal: Aim for a protein source (egg/chicken/curd/dal) in all 4 meals.
🧂
Low oil: Max 1 tsp oil per cooked meal. Grill or boil instead of frying.
🚫
Avoid: Sugary drinks, fried snacks, biscuits, white bread, packaged juice. These are cheap calories with no protein.
😴
No late eating: Finish dinner ~2 hours before bed.
📊
Weigh weekly: Same day, morning, empty stomach. Judge the trend over weeks.

🔄 Cheap Swaps

🐟
Chicken → small fish (sardine/mackerel) when cheaper locally — great protein and omega-3.
🫘
Meat-free day → double the dal + add rajma/chole + paneer 100g for protein.
🥛
Curd → if you can stretch budget, add skimmed milk; cheap protein and calcium.
🌾
Oats → daliya (broken wheat) or poha are cheaper and work the same way.

⚡ On Gym Days vs Rest Days

🏋️
Gym day: Eat the snack (curd + banana + chana) for workout energy. Keep carbs as planned.
🛋️
Rest day: Drop to 1 roti at dinner and skip the banana to create a slightly bigger deficit.
Most important: Consistency beats perfection. Hitting this 6 out of 7 days every week is what drives results — not being flawless for 3 days then quitting.
